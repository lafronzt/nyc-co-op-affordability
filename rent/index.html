This calculator finds the maximum monthly rent you can afford, subject to two constraints:

  • Cash constraint: All move-in costs (deposits + broker fee + fixed fees + post-move reserve) must fit within your available liquid assets.
  • Income constraint: Your annual income must meet the landlord's income multiple (e.g., 40× monthly rent). Optionally, rent must also stay within your DTI limit.

Your maximum rent is the lower of the two constraints. The binding constraint tells you which one is limiting you.

The Afford Target tab lets you reverse the calculation: enter a target rent and see exactly what gaps remain and how to close them.

  • 40× multiplier: Standard NYC landlord requirement (income ≥ 40× monthly rent).
  • Security deposit: NYC rent-stabilized units cap security deposit at 1 month's rent (NY Real Property Law §227-e). Market-rate landlords can require more.
  • Application fee: Capped at $20 in NYC (NYC Admin Code §20-825).
  • Broker fee: Under the FARE Act (effective June 11, 2025), brokers hired by the landlord must be paid by the landlord. Always confirm with your landlord.
  • Renters insurance: Estimate based on NYC average ($12–20/mo for $30k personal property coverage).
  • Market medians: StreetEasy NYC Rental Market Reports (March 2026 data).
