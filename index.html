Two constraints, one limit

This calculator finds the lower of two maximum prices:

  • Cash / Reserves: Your liquid assets must cover the down payment, all closing costs, and a post-close reserve cushion (typically 24 months of carrying costs — maintenance + mortgage). If you don't have enough cash on hand, this constraint binds.
  • DTI / Income: Your total monthly housing payment (mortgage P+I + maintenance + other debts) must stay within the board's debt-to-income ratio limit on your gross monthly income. Most NYC boards use 25–30% DTI.

Whichever constraint produces the lower max price is your binding limit — you can't spend more than both allow simultaneously.

What "Liquidity %" means

Not every dollar in your accounts is freely available. A 401(k) early withdrawal faces income tax plus a 10% penalty — often leaving ~50 cents on the dollar. A Roth IRA has different rules (contributions come out penalty-free, but earnings may not). Set the liquidity % to reflect the actual after-tax, after-penalty dollars you'd actually receive. Accounts at 100% are counted in "Total Liquid." See IRS early-distribution rules for details.

Mansion tax is tiered

New York's mansion tax starts at 1% on purchases of $1M–$2M and steps up to 3.9% above $25M. The 2.5% default assumes a ~$1M purchase. Adjust the variable cost % if your target price moves into a higher tier.

This is an estimate — verify everything

Co-op board requirements vary significantly. Your building may have stricter DTI limits, different reserve requirements, or an unusual flip tax structure. Always confirm numbers with a licensed mortgage broker (for rate locks and DTI qualification) and a real estate attorney (for closing costs and the proprietary lease).
